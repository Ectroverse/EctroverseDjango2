Fleet

In this screen you can organize and manage your fleets. In here you can everything from how many units you have to where your fleets are traveling and how many of them you have as well. Going from top to bottom, left to right here is a description of every function you can see in the fleet screen.

 
First on top Left it shows you what your main fleet is composed of at the moment. Under the main fleet you can see a link to a disband page, on that page you can get rid of any units you do not want or cannot support. Notice that if you have a fleet traveling somewhere, then the units in that fleet won't be shown in the main fleet. Main fleet

On top right are the Fleet orders. The very top of them lets you decided what to do with the fleet after you attack a planet. There are 3 choices in the drop-down menu. The default choice is to station the fleet on that planet (if you are successful in attacking of course). To unstation fleet from the planet, you will have to go to planet screen, click on the planet and then click unstation where the fleet is displayed The second choice is to wait in the system, if you select this instead of being stationed on the planet your fleet will wait in the system for further orders. And last option is to be recalled to main fleet, which will automatically send your fleet back to join the main fleet after the attack.

Under that you can see "Approximations of enemy forces to flee battles", the initial values are as shows at 200%, 200%, 250% and 250%, that means that if the enemy forces for that stage of combat are more that 200% or 250% of your own you will retreat (ex. if you have 100 fighters and your enemy has 200 or more, then you will retreat). If you want to change those values just assign new ones and click on change.

Further down you can see if you have any traveling fleets. There you can see if you have any fleets that are traveling anywhere, if there is more than 1 fleet they will displayed that way and each will have it's own destination and target and ETA (estimated time of arrival). If the fleet has arrived at it's destination instead of ETA you will see a link "Ready to attack" (exploration ships are an exception to that, they explore automatically when they get to their target). Click on the link to see the outcome of the battle. In details you can recall your fleet back as well as give them a different order.

Lastly at the very bottom you can see your main fleet, there you can dispatch a new fleet to a different target, simply type in how many of each unit you want to send, then select the order you want to give (you can chose from attack the planet, station on planet and move to system) and type in the coordinate of the planet or system and press "Send". The fleet will appear in "traveling fleets" the next time you load the fleet page.
